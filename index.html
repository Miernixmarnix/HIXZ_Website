Natural Bridge & Blackstone Beach

Explore the rugged north coast where the ocean has carved spectacular formations out of coral rock. The trail to Blackstone Beach is a hidden gem for those looking to see Aruba's wilder side.

Arikok National Park

Nearly 20% of the island is a protected sanctuary. From the Spooky Trail's mysterious atmosphere to the peak of Jamanota, Arikok offers the most diverse hiking trails on the island.

Ayo & Casibari Rock Formations

Wander through massive monolithic boulders and ancient Arawak petroglyphs. These sites offer paved paths that make for an easy but mystical walk through Aruba's inland history.

  • Hidden Gems

    Beyond the Natural Bridge lies the 'Small Natural Pool.' It's a bit of a climb down a ladder, but it's much quieter than the tourist spots.

  • Early Starts

    The Aruban sun is no joke. I always start my treks by 6:30 AM to catch the sunrise and finish before the midday heat hits 30°C.

  • Offline Maps

    Cell service in the heart of Arikok is spotty. I recommend downloading the area on AllTrails or Google Maps before you head out.

  • Safety First

    The terrain is volcanic and sharp. I never hike without sturdy boots and at least 2 liters of water. Safety is the key to a good adventure.

  • Spooky Trail

    One of my favorites! It winds through ancient Cacti forests. It’s quiet, mystical, and feels like you’ve stepped back in time.

  • Capture the View

    The limestone cliffs near the North Coast offer the best photo ops. Just watch your step—the wind can be surprisingly strong!
